Data Modifications I Made to make the data more deceptive:

Four data points were modified. The 2018 count was raised from 35 to 52 to remove an early dip and fake a smooth upward trend from the start. Req 2: Chart Checklist

  • Does y axis start at zero? If not, probably manipulation going on. Check y axis.
  • Are the axis scales consistent? If there are dual axis make sure the units cover the same space and it is not a situation where non-trending data is made to look like it is highly correlated
  • Is the time range subset that has a unique pattern? Maybe the author picked a time range that is uncharacteristcally different from the norm.
  • Are the colors associated with "emotional" responses. If the author is using red or green they may be trying to trick the readers brain into thinking something is good or bad innately.
  • Are there erroneous annotations or annotations that suggest a certain conclusion to the reader? If so, then there is a chance that the publisher has a message they want to push.
  • Is the data population/size stated? If not there may be few points and trend lines may not tell the true story of the data.

Red Flags of AI Generated Charts

  • No Citations: AI tools routinely generate plausible-sounding journal names, authors, or report titles that do not exist. Always check the source independently before using a chart.
  • Perfect data trending: Real-world data is noisy and fluctuates. If an AI-generated chart shows a perfectly smooth curve with no variance, the data may have been smoothed or fake altogether
  • No pointer to the raw data: If the AI only shows a chart image with no reference to the base dataset, there is no way to validate the graphs and it is probably AI.
